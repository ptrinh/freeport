Delete your Freeport account

Freeport (developer: Phil T · uk.trinh.freeport)

Freeport is a self-custodial, peer-to-peer app. There is no Freeport server that holds an account for you: your "account" is a cryptographic key (a Nostr identity) generated and stored on your own device, together with your local settings, saved addresses, messages, and — if you enabled the wallet — your wallet keys. We do not have a database of users to delete from.

Delete everything, in the app

You can permanently erase your account and all of its data directly in the app, with no request and no waiting:

  1. Open Freeport.
  2. Go to the Settings tab.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and tap Delete account.
  4. Confirm. This immediately and irreversibly wipes the identity key, profile, settings, address book, messages, wallet keys, and all other local data from the device.
This cannot be undone. Because Freeport is self-custodial, no one — including the developer — can recover a deleted key. If you might want your identity or funds back later, export a backup before deleting (Settings → back up your identity).

What is deleted

  • Deleted immediately: everything stored on your device — your key/identity, display name and profile, phone number (kept local only), saved addresses, chat history, and wallet keys.
  • Cloud backup (if you created one): a backup you saved to iCloud/Google or a file is under your control — delete that file to remove it.
  • Public events already broadcast to Nostr relays (e.g. listings you posted) are outside any single party's control and may persist on relays you do not operate. Listings carry an expiration tag (NIP-40), but each relay decides how long to keep data. Treat anything you published publicly as potentially permanent.

Prefer to uninstall?

Uninstalling the app also removes the local copy of your data from the device. Using Delete account first is the thorough option, as it clears the secure keystore entries explicitly.

Need help?

If you can't access the app but want confirmation that no server-side account exists for you (it doesn't), email privacy@freeport.network.
